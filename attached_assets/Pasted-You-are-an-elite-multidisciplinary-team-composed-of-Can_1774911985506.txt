You are an elite multidisciplinary team composed of:

* Canadian Market Research Analyst
* Product Manager (Fintech / SME / SaaS)
* Behavioral Economist
* UX Researcher
* Policy & Compliance Expert (Canada)
* Growth Strategist

Your task is to RE-THINK and EXPAND a product/system specifically for the CANADIAN market.

You must:

* Ground all thinking in real Canadian behavior, regulations, and infrastructure
* Use research-based reasoning (not assumptions)
* Identify hidden problems users themselves may not articulate
* Propose innovative, high-impact features

---

## 1. CANADIAN CONTEXT ANALYSIS

Analyze how Canada differs from emerging markets:

* Banking system (Interac, credit cards, loans)
* Regulations (taxation, privacy laws like PIPEDA)
* Consumer expectations (trust, UX quality, speed)
* Digital literacy (generally high but diverse)
* Cost of living pressures
* Multicultural population

Identify:

* What assumptions from other markets (e.g., East Africa) DO NOT apply
* What new constraints or opportunities exist

---

## 2. USER SEGMENT DEEP DIVE

Break down Canadian users into segments such as:

* Small business owners (retail, services)
* Gig workers / freelancers
* Immigrants / newcomers
* Students
* Corporate users

For each segment:

* Identify daily workflows
* Pain points (financial, operational, emotional)
* Frustrations with existing tools

---

## 3. CURRENT SOLUTIONS & GAPS

Evaluate tools currently used in Canada:

* Accounting (e.g., QuickBooks alternatives)
* Payments (Interac, Stripe)
* POS systems
* CRM tools

Identify:

* Where users are underserved
* What is too complex, expensive, or fragmented
* What users tolerate but hate

---

## 4. HIDDEN PROBLEM DISCOVERY (CRITICAL)

Identify problems users may NOT explicitly say but experience:

Examples:

* Cognitive overload from too many tools
* Fear of tax mistakes
* Lack of real-time financial clarity
* Poor integration between systems
* Trust issues with automation

You must surface at least:

* Emotional problems
* Operational inefficiencies
* Financial blind spots

---

## 5. FEATURE INNOVATION (MANDATORY)

Propose at least 5 HIGH-IMPACT, NON-OBVIOUS features tailored for Canada.

Each feature must:

* Solve a real problem identified above
* Be specific and actionable (not vague ideas)
* Include how it works (step-by-step)
* Explain why competitors are not doing it well

Examples of acceptable depth:

* Not “AI assistant”
* But: “Real-time GST/HST auto-calculation + filing assistant integrated with CRA workflows”

---

## 6. COMPLIANCE & TRUST LAYER

Evaluate:

* Privacy expectations (PIPEDA)
* Financial compliance (CRA, taxes)
* Data security expectations

Design features that:

* Increase trust
* Make compliance effortless
* Reduce user anxiety

---

## 7. UX EXPECTATION SHIFT

Canadian users expect:

* Clean UI
* Speed
* Reliability
* Transparency

Audit:

* What must be redesigned to meet this bar
* What would feel “low quality” or untrustworthy

---

## 8. COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE

Identify:

* How this product can win in Canada
* What unique angle it can take (e.g., immigrant-first, AI-first, compliance-first)

---

## 9. FINAL OUTPUT

Provide:

1. Key Canadian Insights
2. Top 5 Hidden Problems
3. 5+ Innovative Features (detailed)
4. Why competitors are missing these
5. Risks in entering Canada
6. Go-to-market considerations
7. Product positioning for Canada

---

## RULES

* Be specific to Canada (no generic global answers)
* Avoid obvious or surface-level ideas
* Focus on real-world behavior and systems
* Prioritize trust, compliance, and usability

Your mission is to transform this product into something that feels:
BUILT FOR CANADA — not adapted to it.
